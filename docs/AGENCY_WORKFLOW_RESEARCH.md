# Agency Workflow — Deep Research (Jun 9 2026)

> **Purpose (decided with Gaurav):** dual-use. (1) Our operating manual for running OffGrid as a
> real full-service agency, AND (2) the blueprint that Grid Control's 18 agents must automate.
> Scope = full-service (onboarding → strategy → organic + paid + funnel + email + web → reporting →
> retention → scale). Sources weighted across operator agencies, AI-native agencies, creator/founder
> playbooks, and tooling research. This is research, not a build instruction.

There are **two distinct workflows** every agency runs, and people conflate them. Grid Control must
serve both:

- **Workflow A — the agency's OWN growth engine** (how WE/clients get clients): positioning → lead
  gen → sales → close. This is also UC3 (a user runs their own agency on Grid Control).
- **Workflow B — client delivery** (how we grow the brand we onboarded): onboard → discover →
  deliver → report → retain → expand. This is UC1/UC2.

Grid Control's agents already map mostly to Workflow B. Workflow A is under-built today (dm-hunter +
funnel-specialist are the seeds). The research below covers both, then maps to our roster.

## PART A — WORKFLOW A: How agencies win their own clients

### A1. Positioning & offer (the precondition)
- **Specialists beat generalists in 2026.** Agencies focused on a niche industry or single service
  generate leads more effectively — they speak to specific pain. "The agencies growing fastest aren't
  generalists — they're specialists."
- **Productized offer > custom retainer.** A productized service = a retainer with public pricing and
  a fixed scope. Milestone-based, fixed packages reduce buyer risk and speed decisions.
- **Hormozi value equation** (how much you can charge):
  `Price = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice)`.
  Maximise dream outcome + perceived likelihood; minimise time-to-result + effort required of client.
- **Hormozi "Fulfill & Integrate" alt model** (lean, 7-figure with small team): fulfill results
  FIRST → record training (2 days) → transfer via access + 4 weekly coaching calls. Tiering: ~12 core
  done-for-you clients · ~50 training clients · ~1,000 product buyers. Relevant to our 3 products
  (Reporting Project = product tier; Grid Control services = core tier).

### A2. Lead generation (two engines, run both)
- **Inbound** (cheaper, compounding trust): content marketing, SEO/GEO, organic social. Treat
  IG/LinkedIn/TikTok as **search engines** — make content for what the ICP already searches.
- **Outbound** (faster, direct): cold email, LinkedIn DMs (single best B2B channel for agency lead-gen
  in 2026), cold calling, paid ads.
- **Run your own ads as proof** — a small Google/Meta campaign that shows the ROI you got for
  yourself is the highest-trust outbound asset.
- **Lead magnets that work in 2026 = diagnostic tools** (scorecards, audits) that surface a specific
  problem and position you as the fixer. ← This is exactly the Reporting Project's wedge.
- **Referrals = 40–60% of agency new business**, but most agencies have NO system — they just hope.
  Formalise: ask at value-moments, reward validated referrals.
- **MQL vs SQL:** MQL = downloaded a magnet / attended webinar; SQL = vetted, confirmed budget +
  timeline. Prioritise lead QUALITY over quantity (agency sales cycles are long).

### A3. The sales process (call → discovery → audit → proposal → close)
Universal pattern: **call → discovery → presentation → close.**
- **Discovery** = 1 call or a series (45–60 min each). Uncover: expensive problem, desired outcome,
  what they've already tried, decision process, budget/timeline, fit.
- **Audit-led selling** is the highest-converting motion: an audit makes the prospect's real problems
  visible, builds value, gives them a vision, validates your pitch. ← Reporting Project = a paid/lead
  audit that ascends into core services. "Foot in the door with a lower-priced service → quick win →
  ascend."
- **Get verbal approval before building the detailed proposal** — don't spec everything only to have
  scope/price cut.
- **Reality of close rates:** most agencies close only **10–15%** of high-ticket retainer calls;
  **20–27%** with properly qualified leads. Smaller retainers close in 4–8 weeks; large ones, months.

### A4. Pricing models
- **Monthly retainer** — most popular 2026, ~$1k–$25k+/mo. Predictable, ongoing optimisation.
- **Fixed project fee** — defined deliverable.
- **Performance-based** — tied to results (risky; usually blended with a base).
- **Productized** — retainer with public price + fixed scope (modern rate card).
- Our two-tier maps cleanly: **Reporting Project** (₹2.5–7k, productized intelligence/audit, lead
  magnet + ascension) → **Grid Control services** (₹15–50k, core done-for-you retainer).

## PART B — WORKFLOW B: Client delivery lifecycle

### B1. Onboarding (the first 90 days set the whole relationship)
**The first 90 days determine relationship trajectory; strong onboarding measurably lifts retention.**
Canonical 7-step (AgencyAnalytics) + composite of others:

1. **Contract & payment** — signed contract (DocuSign/HelloSign), payment method, deposit upfront.
2. **Internal kickoff** — align team, assign account manager/roles, share client info (site, handles,
   contacts, logins, collateral) before facing the client.
3. **Onboarding questionnaire** — 15–20 questions, deadline (~10 business days), support contact.
   Captures: business, audience, tone/voice, goals, KPIs, past marketing results, competitors,
   what they've tried. (Keep ≤15 if possible — short, clear.)
4. **Technical setup & access collection** — website creds, analytics (GA4), ad accounts (Meta/Google),
   Search Console, CRM, proprietary tools. Connect everything to reporting + project management.
   (Leadsie-style permission-request flows reduce friction.)
5. **Reporting setup** — identify top KPIs, build report template(s), set cadence/format (live
   dashboard vs periodic), assign who writes commentary, automate where possible.
6. **Client launch meeting (kickoff)** — introduce teams, review questionnaire, address concerns,
   define 30/60/90 tasks, agree KPIs + how measured + how you'll communicate, share PM tool access.
   Follow up with a written summary email.
7. **Scope-creep prevention** — tight SOW with explicit limits, a standardised change-request form,
   regular check-ins that tie back to original scope. Flag "Phase 2" upsell items spotted in
   questionnaire.

Timeline: onboarding typically spans **2–4 weeks**.

### B2. Discovery / audit / strategy phase (before any campaign)
A real discovery runs **5–7 business days**. Deliverables:
- **Audience/persona development** — from existing data, team interviews, mini focus groups: who they
  are, where they are, how they talk, how to speak to them.
- **Three-discipline audit** — (a) technical website review, (b) ads + conversion-readiness review of
  paid accounts and their destinations, (c) strategic review of competitive landscape + market
  opportunity.
- **Messaging framework** — how to talk to each persona about the brand + each initiative.
- **Content strategy** — what to create/repurpose across channels.
- **Systems diagnosis** — how CRM/CMS/email/analytics should work together.
- **Final deliverable = a written strategy doc** naming what's working, what's broken, the
  highest-leverage moves, and an advertising plan (platforms, creative, flight pacing vs budget/goals).

### B3. Delivery engines (run per channel, continuously)

**Organic content engine (GaryVee pillar model — already our doctrine):**
- One **pillar** (long-form: keynote/vlog/podcast/YouTube) → repurpose into dozens of
  platform-native derivatives. Tier 1 pillar → Tier 2 derivatives (blogs, email, carousels) → Tier 3
  micro (clips, quotes, data callouts, posts).
- **Rule of thirds** (Meta): ⅓ value/awareness, ⅓ interaction/shared, ⅓ promotional.
- **2026 ranking reality:** saves, shares, substantive comments, DMs, watch-time, rewatches >> likes.
  (Confirms our save-to-impression KPI focus.)
- Ship short-form where the audience is (Reels/TikTok/Shorts); re-title for YouTube Shorts search to
  win discovery. Build a **documented repurposing engine** that auto-triggers derivatives from every
  pillar.

**Content production workflow (7 stages):** ideation → planning (calendar) → production →
review/approval → optimization → publishing/distribution → tracking. Define who creates / who reviews
/ who approves, with set approval timelines so the schedule never slips. Unify briefs + PM + approvals
+ reporting in ONE system (disconnected tools is the #1 workflow blocker).

**Paid ads engine (performance):**
- Every engagement starts with **research → structured testing → rapid optimization** across ads, CRO,
  messaging, analytics. Unified system: paid acquisition + CRO + landing pages + analytics.
- **Creative is the lever.** Test **15–20 creative variants/month**; winners drop CPA hard. UGC
  (unboxing, honest reviews, before/after) drives ~29% higher conversion than brand-produced.
- **Full-funnel:** UGC for TOFU awareness, testimonials/use-case for MOFU social proof, offer/retargeting
  for BOFU. Feed 3–5 fresh creatives into the existing campaign (don't reset the algo's learning).
  Use Advantage+/automation to scale.

**Funnel / lead-gen engine (for the client):** lead magnet (diagnostic/scorecard) → landing page →
opt-in → 5–7 email nurture → booked call. Hot leads: contact within 15 min.

**Community / DM engine (where service brands actually convert):**
- Community management ≠ marketing. Marketing brings them in; community keeps them, makes advocates.
- "The comment is the content" — proactive engagement is a growth channel, not janitor work.
- **DMs convert better than link-traffic for many service businesses.** Reach out to repeat engagers;
  value-first, conversational, never pitch-first; transition naturally to a discovery call.

**Web/SEO/GEO engine:** technical SEO + content + **GEO** (get cited by AI engines): dominate
AI-trusted directories (Clutch, G2), seed Reddit/Quora, get into third-party listicles LLMs cite.

### B4. Reporting (the #1 retention driver)
Cadence + format scale with stakes:
- **Weekly** — simple email, pulse on key metrics.
- **Monthly** — hybrid report + video (Loom-style) walkthrough; web analytics + campaign performance.
- **QBR (quarterly)** — **always a live conversation**, never an emailed PDF. Covers: what was
  achieved, what the data showed, competitive landscape, concrete next-quarter plan. One of the
  highest-value retention touchpoints + the natural upsell moment.
- **Every report:** executive summary, 5–10 KPIs tied to the client's actual business goals, context
  per metric, what drove results, specific next-step recommendations. (Numbers alone ≠ insight.)

### B5. Retention & account management
- **Retaining a client costs 5–7× less than replacing one.** Each churned client = 3–5 lost referrals.
- **Why clients leave:** lack of proactive strategic guidance (**68%**), poor communication/transparency
  (**57%**). 80% rank "an honest relationship" as the most important agency behavior.
- **Account manager = single point of contact**, proactive, builds rapport → longer tenure + more
  revenue. Retention IS the growth strategy.

### B6. Expansion — upsell / cross-sell / referrals
- Existing clients are the easiest sale. **QBRs are the upsell venue** (prove value → expand scope).
- Upsell = premium tier; cross-sell = add a service (e.g. organic client adds paid, or adds email).
- Mature upsell/cross-sell systems → **2.8× higher retention, +43% revenue/account** (HBR). Only 34%
  of orgs have the tooling though — gap = opportunity.
- **Formalise referrals** (40–60% of new business): ask at value-moments, reward validated referrals.

### B7. Offboarding (do it gracefully — it drives referrals + win-backs)
Clean handover of assets/access, final report, exit conversation (learn why), keep the door open.
(Lightly covered in sources; standard practice — protects reputation + future referrals.)

- Small agencies → all-in-one; 20+ people → best-of-breed that integrates.

> + automation), with LIVE auto-tracked metrics — vs the fragmented stack above and Sean Purvis's
> MANUAL metrics. That's the wedge.

## PART D — The AI-native agency model (where we actually compete)
From McKinsey + the agentic-agency implementation guides:
- **Agentic AI will power ~⅔ of current marketing activities**; expect **10–30% revenue growth** from
  hyper-personalization and **10–15× faster** campaign creation/execution. But **<10% of CMOs have
  deployed end-to-end workflows that generate measurable value** — the field is wide open.
- **Reference architecture (Refresh Agent):** n8n (orchestration) + Claude (reasoning) + MCP
  (connectivity) + Apify (scraping) + Perplexity (research) + GA4/GSC (data). Our stack is the same
  shape minus n8n (we orchestrate in Python via CEO Brain + the planned LiteLLM gateway + APScheduler).
- **Two flagship automated workflows worth copying:**
  1. **Local Niche Research → lead-gen** (Workflow A): Apify scrapes Google Maps businesses → filter
     for "high demand / low saturation" + "reputation arbitrage" → Claude mines negative reviews for
     pain → auto-generate a personalized lead magnet per prospect. *Replaces ~20 hrs/market with ~1 hr.*
     → This is a concrete new capability for our dm-hunter + funnel-specialist.
  2. **Search-Everywhere Content Engine** (Workflow B): one keyword/video in → research (Perplexity)
     → transcription → gap analysis → out: blog + thread + carousel + newsletter + calendar, with a
     human-review pause node. → Maps to trend-researcher → content-planner → script/carousel.
- **Agent autonomy levels (L1–L4):** assistive → orchestrated → agentic (goal-directed + tools) →
  autonomous (self-improving + memory). Our Trust Dial = exposing L1→L4 per brand.
- **Centaur model (what stays human):** strategic vision / "vibe setting", picking the winning angle
  from AI variants, approval gates, governance (hallucination + prompt-injection + cost), client
  relationships. Agents own ~80% of logistics. ← Matches our approval-gate + zero-assumption doctrine.
- **Governance the guides insist on (we already have most):** outputs must cite verified sources
  (= our Rule 10 data_provenance); prompt-injection allowlists (= our untrusted-content law); plan
  mode / blocked sensitive actions; full audit logs; token dashboards (= our cost panel wedge).
- **AI-native pricing trend:** outcome-based + "agentic readiness audits" + "vibe strategy" consulting
  packages — premium positioning beyond hourly.

### What this research says we're MISSING (implications for the build — not a build instruction)
1. **A sales/closing layer (A3)** and **upsell/referral layer (B6)** — Workflow A is thin. The
   Reporting Project (audit-as-lead-magnet) is the right wedge; needs a prospecting→audit→nurture flow.
2. **The Local-Niche-Research lead-gen workflow** — concrete, high-ROI capability for dm-hunter +
   funnel (Apify → filter → review-mining → personalized magnet).
3. **Reporting CADENCE engine** — weekly email / monthly report+video / live QBR, not just metrics.
   Every report needs exec summary + insight + recommendation, not raw numbers (ties to our no-JSON,
   human-readable rule).
4. **Proactive strategic guidance = the #1 retention move (68% churn cause).** The Chief-of-Staff
   daily brief is literally the fix — this research validates the dashboard paradigm.
5. **Scope-creep / change-request discipline** in onboarding (SOW + change form).
6. **GEO** (AI-citation) belongs in seo-aeo's mandate explicitly.

## Key numbers to remember
- Retention costs **5–7×** less than acquisition; churn loses **3–5** referrals each.
- Churn causes: **68%** lack of proactive guidance, **57%** poor communication.
- Referrals = **40–60%** of agency new business (usually unsystematized).
- High-ticket close rate: **10–15%** (cold) → **20–27%** (qualified).
- Creative testing: **15–20 variants/month**; UGC ≈ **+29%** conversion.
- Mature upsell systems: **2.8×** retention, **+43%** revenue/account.
- Agentic AI: **~⅔** of marketing activities, **10–30%** revenue lift, **10–15×** faster, but **<10%**
  of CMOs have shipped measurable end-to-end workflows.
